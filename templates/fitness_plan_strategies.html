Weight Loss — 12-week Progressive

Weight Loss — Full Details

This sample 12-week plan focuses on sustainable fat loss. Calorie deficit, progressive strength work to preserve muscle, and cardio to boost energy expenditure.

Sample Week (beginner)

DaySession
Mon30 min brisk walk + 20 min bodyweight strength (squat, pushup, plank)
Tue30 min low-impact cardio (bike/walk)
WedRest / Mobility
ThuIntervals: 6×1min run / 2 min walk
FriFull body strength 30–40 min
SatActive recovery: yoga or long walk
SunRest

Nutrition

Aim for a moderate calorie deficit (~300 kcal/day). Prioritise protein (1.4–2.0 g/kg), fibre, and whole foods. Example plate: grilled protein + veg + complex carb.

Safety & Tips

  • Start at a comfortable intensity — progress gradually.
  • Track weight & measurements weekly — focus on trends, not day-to-day.
  • Consult a doctor if you have medical conditions before starting.

Muscle Building — Full Details

A sample 16-week hypertrophy block. Emphasises progressive overload, training frequency and sufficient calories.

Sample 4-week microcycle (Upper/Lower)

  • Mon (Upper): Bench press 4x6–8, Row 4x8, Shoulder press 3x8–10, Arms
  • Tue (Lower): Squat 4x6–8, Romanian deadlift 3x8, Lunges, Calves
  • Thu (Upper): Pull-ups, Incline DB press, Face pulls
  • Fri (Lower): Deadlift variations, Leg press, Hamstring focus

Nutrition & Recovery

Eat in a small surplus (300–500 kcal), prioritise protein and sleep. Use progressive overload (track sets × reps × weight).

HIIT & Conditioning

Short, intense sessions to improve VO2 and burn calories in less time. Not recommended daily — use 2–3 times per week.

Sample Tabata

20s work / 10s rest — 8 rounds.
Exercises: Jump squat, burpees, mountain climbers, high knees.
Full session: 16–20 minutes including warm-up & cool-down.

Nutrition Quick Guide

Protein: 1.4–2.2 g/kg depending on goal. Carbs: Timing around workouts. Fats: 20–30% of calories.

Frequently Asked

How fast will I see results?
Depends on goal; fat loss is typically visible after 4–6 weeks of consistent nutrition and training.
Do I need a gym?
No — several plans are home-friendly. For hypertrophy, gym access helps but bodyweight progressions work too.
