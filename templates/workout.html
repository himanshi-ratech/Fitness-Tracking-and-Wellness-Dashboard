Full-Body Gym Workouts — Programs for Men & Women (By Age)

Equipment-based full-body workouts, warm-ups, mobility, running suggestions and cool-downs. Choose gender + age group + intensity level for a practical, progressive plan.

Men — Age Groups

Age 18–30 — Beginner / Intermediate / Advanced

Focus: Strength, hypertrophy, conditioning

Warm-up (10–12 minutes)

  • 5 min light treadmill / stationary bike jog
  • Dynamic mobility — leg swings, arm circles, world's greatest stretch (6–8 reps)
  • Warm-up sets for compound lifts

Full Body (Beginner — 3 days/week)

  • Squat (Goblet or Barbell) — 3×8–10
  • Bench Press — 3×8–10
  • Bent-over Row — 3×8–10
  • Romanian Deadlift — 3×8–10
  • Plank — 3×45–60s

Progressions (Intermediate / Advanced)

  • Increase to 4–5 sets, change rep ranges (5–8 heavy, 8–12 hypertrophy)
  • Add accessory: lunges, pull-ups, overhead press, weighted carries
  • Introduce sprint intervals or 20–30 min steady-state cardio once weekly

Cool-down & Stretching

  • 5–8 min light cycling/walking
  • Static stretches for quads, hamstrings, chest, lats (30s each)

Age 31–45 — Maintain Strength & Mobility

Focus: joint health, sustainable strength

Warm-up

  • 5–8 min brisk walk / row
  • Band pull-aparts, hip openers, thoracic rotations

Full Body (3–4 days/week)

  • Front Squat or Goblet — 4×6–8
  • Incline Dumbbell Press — 4×8–10
  • Single-arm Row — 3×10 each
  • Hip Thrust — 3×8–12
  • Farmer Carry — 3×40m

Recovery & Running

  • 2–3 easy runs (20–35 min) or 1 interval session (6×400m)
  • Priority on sleep, mobility and foam rolling

Age 46+ — Strength & Preserve Function

Focus: balance, joint care, lean muscle preservation

Warm-up

  • 6–10 min elliptical / brisk walk
  • Gentle mobility: ankle circles, shoulder band work, hip hinges

Full Body (2–3 days/week)

  • Goblet Squat — 3×8–12
  • Seated Row / TRX Row — 3×8–12
  • Step-ups or Split Squat — 3×8 each
  • Romanian Deadlift (light) — 3×8–10
  • Core: Deadbug / Bird Dog — 3×10 each

Cardio & Mobility

  • Low impact cardio 3×30 min (bike/walk/swim)
  • Daily mobility and balance drills

Women — Age Groups

Age 18–30 — Strength & Conditioning

Focus: compound strength, glute & posterior chain

Warm-up

  • 5–8 min treadmill incline walk or bike
  • Glute activation: banded lateral walks, clams

Full Body (3 days/week)

  • Hip Thrust — 4×8–12
  • Goblet Squat — 3×10–12
  • Push-ups / Incline Press — 3×8–12
  • Single-leg Romanian Deadlift — 3×8 each
  • Core: Plank variations — 3×45s

Progressions

  • Increase load or switch to barbell variations; add accessory glute work and single-leg strength

Age 31–45 — Strength & Balance

Focus: maintain strength while managing life demands

Full Body (3–4 days/week)

  • Deadlift (conventional/hex) — 3×5–8
  • DB Bench Press — 3×8–10
  • Bulgarian Split Squat — 3×8 each
  • Pull-downs / Assisted Pull-ups — 3×8–10

Cardio & Recovery

  • Moderate runs or interval walks 2×/week; yoga/mobility once a week

Age 46+ — Strength & Functional Fitness

Focus: bone health, balance and functional movement

Full Body (2–3 days/week)

  • Leg Press / Goblet Squat — 3×10
  • Seated Row — 3×10
  • Glute Bridge — 3×12
  • Balance drills & core work

Cardio

  • Low impact activities: walking, elliptical, swimming — 3×30 min

Unisex / Both Genders — Templates & Guidance

General Full Body Template (3 days/week)

Choose these if you want a balanced approach regardless of gender.

  • Day A: Squat, Bench, Row, Core — 3–4 sets
  • Day B: Deadlift, Overhead Press, Pull-ups/Lat pulldown — 3–4 sets
  • Day C: Accessory & Conditioning — lunges, RDLs, sled work or 20–30 min cardio

Programming & Safety Notes

  • Progressive overload is key — add small weight increments weekly or increase reps.
  • Prioritise form over load. Use a spotter for heavy lifts.
  • Deload every 6–8 weeks if training intensely.
  • Consult a doctor before starting if you have medical conditions.
